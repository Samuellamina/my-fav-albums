MY FAVOURITE ALBUMS THIS YEAR

1

Harry Styles - Fine Line

Harry Styles’s Fine Line Eschews Boundaries for Youthful Self-Discovery







2

Travis Scott - Astroworld

Travis Scott’s third album is inarguably his strongest to date.
His skill as a curator helps sculpt a sticky, humid, psychedelic
world with dazzling production and odd pleasures at every turn.







3

Ariana Grande - My Everything

On My Everything, Ariana Grande ditches the manic-Disney-dream-girl
ballads that made up her debut and goes straight for the bangers.







4

Slowthai - Tyron

The UK rapper’s first album since his disastrous appearance
at last year’s NME Awards is split between welcome introspection
and all-caps boasting. It’s a refreshingly candid self-assessment.







5

Ghetts - Conflict of Interest

2021's Best Album.
‘Conflict Of Interest’ could sit on the same shelf as Dave’s ‘Psychodrama’
as an album that depicts honest tales of London through the art of
true lyricism, a tradition that will never die out.







6

Lizzo - Cuz I Love You

On her major-label debut Cuz I Love You, Lizzo takes all her strengths
to the next level, and the results are her most consistent,
and consistently joyous, set of songs yet.







7

J Hus - Big Conspiracy

The British rapper barely puts a foot wrong on introspective second album
With club-ready tracks sharing space with hard-won wisdom,
J Hus' second album is a brilliant, career-defining collection.







8

Skepta - Konnichiwa

On Skepta's long-awaited new album,
the grime pioneer is full of sneering contempt for popular
culture’s industry of image, the press, the police, and the government at large.







9

Ajebutter 22 - What Happens in Lagos

What Happens in Lagos is Ajebutter22’s best work till date
and at this level he has managed to drag himself while keeping a day job,
we can’t doubt his ability to surpass this body of work.







10

J. Cole - The Off-Season

The Off-Season — an earnest return to blood,
sweat and ink — doesn't need much more to hit like swish.
